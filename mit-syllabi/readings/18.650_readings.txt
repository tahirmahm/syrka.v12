=== READING LIST BY LECTURE ===
Lec 1–2: Introduction — parametric models, exponential families, identifiability
Lec 3: Parametric estimation — method of moments
Lec 4–5: Maximum likelihood estimation — consistency, asymptotic normality
Lec 6: Fisher information and Cramér-Rao lower bound
Lec 7–10: Hypothesis testing — Type I/II errors, p-values, level and power
Lec 10–11: Wald test, likelihood ratio test, chi-squared test
Lec 11–12: Goodness of fit — chi-squared GOF, Kolmogorov-Smirnov
Lec 13–14: Regression — simple OLS, R², residual diagnostics
Lec 15–16: Multiple regression — matrix form, F-test, AIC/BIC
Lec 17–18: Bayesian statistics — prior, posterior, conjugate priors
Lec 18–19: Bayesian inference — MAP, credible intervals, MCMC intro
Lec 19–20: PCA — covariance matrix, eigendecomposition
Lec 21–24: GLMs — logistic regression, Poisson regression, link functions

=== KEY CONCEPTS ===
Parametric models, MLE consistency and asymptotic normality, Fisher information,
Cramér-Rao bound, hypothesis testing, p-values, goodness-of-fit, OLS regression,
multiple regression, Bayesian inference, conjugate priors, PCA, GLMs
