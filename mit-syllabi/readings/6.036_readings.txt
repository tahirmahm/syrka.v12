=== TEXTBOOKS ===
Course notes on site (no required textbook).

=== READING LIST BY WEEK ===
Wk 1: Introduction — supervised vs unsupervised, hypothesis class, bias-variance
Wk 2: Linear classifiers — perceptron, convergence theorem, geometric interpretation
Wk 3: Logistic regression — cross-entropy loss, gradient descent, probabilistic view
Wk 4: Regression — OLS, ridge, lasso, elastic net, regularisation theory
Wk 5: Neural networks I — feedforward, activation functions (ReLU, sigmoid, tanh)
Wk 6: Neural networks II — backpropagation derivation via chain rule
Wk 7: CNNs — convolutional layers, pooling, batch norm, dropout, architectures
Wk 8: Deep learning — transfer learning, data augmentation, training tricks
Wk 9: RNNs — sequence modelling, LSTM gates, GRU, language modelling
Wk 10: MDPs — states, actions, rewards, discount factor, policy
Wk 11: Reinforcement learning — Q-learning, Bellman equation, value iteration
Wk 12: Decision trees — CART, information gain, Gini impurity, bagging
Wk 13: Clustering — k-means, Gaussian mixture models, EM algorithm
Wk 14: Unsupervised — autoencoders, PCA, t-SNE, dimensionality reduction

=== KEY CONCEPTS ===
Bias-variance tradeoff, perceptron convergence theorem, cross-entropy loss,
gradient descent variants (SGD, mini-batch), L1/L2 regularisation, backpropagation
chain rule derivation, convolutional weight sharing, LSTM gate mechanism,
Bellman optimality, CART splitting criteria, EM algorithm for GMMs
