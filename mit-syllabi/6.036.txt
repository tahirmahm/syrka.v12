MIT 6.036 — Introduction to Machine Learning
Source: MIT OpenCourseWare
URL: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-036-introduction-to-machine-learning-fall-2020/

=== COURSE DESCRIPTION ===
This course introduces principles, algorithms, and applications of machine learning from the point of view of modeling and prediction. It covers supervised learning (classification and regression), unsupervised learning, and reinforcement learning. The course includes formulation of learning problems, representation, optimization, and generalization, with topics including linear classifiers, perceptrons, neural networks, support vector machines, decision trees, k-nearest neighbours, clustering, and dimensionality reduction.

Students gain both theoretical understanding of machine learning methods and practical experience implementing algorithms in Python.

=== LECTURE TOPICS ===
1. Introduction to Machine Learning
2. Perceptrons and Linear Classifiers
3. Features and Feature Engineering
4. Margin Maximization and Regularization
5. Regression: Linear Regression
6. Gradient Descent Optimization
7. Stochastic Gradient Descent
8. Neural Networks: Forward Propagation
9. Neural Networks: Backpropagation
10. Convolutional Neural Networks
11. Recurrent Neural Networks
12. State Machines and Markov Decision Processes
13. Reinforcement Learning: Value Iteration
14. Reinforcement Learning: Q-Learning
15. Decision Trees and Random Forests
16. Recommender Systems and Collaborative Filtering
