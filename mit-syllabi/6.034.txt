MIT 6.034 — Artificial Intelligence
Source: MIT OpenCourseWare
URL: https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/6-034-artificial-intelligence-fall-2010/

=== COURSE DESCRIPTION ===
This course introduces students to the basic knowledge representation, problem solving, and learning methods of artificial intelligence. Upon completion, students should be able to develop intelligent systems by assembling solutions to concrete computational problems; understand the role of knowledge representation, problem solving, and learning in intelligent-system engineering; and appreciate the role of problem solving, vision, and language in understanding human intelligence.

The course covers a wide range of classical AI techniques including search algorithms, constraint satisfaction, game playing, logical reasoning, neural networks, and genetic algorithms, providing a comprehensive foundation in AI methodology.

6. Search: A* and Games (Minimax, Alpha-Beta)
7. Constraints: Interpreting Line Drawings
8. Constraints: Search and Domain Reduction
9. Constraints: Visual Object Recognition
10. Introduction to Learning
11. Learning: Identification Trees and Disorder
12. Learning: Neural Nets
13. Learning: Neural Nets (continued) and Backpropagation
14. Learning: Genetic Algorithms
15. Learning: Sparse Spaces and Phonology
16. Learning: Near Misses and Felicity Conditions
17. Learning: Support Vector Machines
18. Learning: Boosting
19. Representations: Classes, Trajectories, Transitions
20. Architectures: GPS, SOAR, Subsumption, Society of Mind
